### Requirements

* [Transparent Dark Grey Acrylic Perspex Plastic](https://www.ebay.com.au/itm/300906936180?siteid=15), ie. `A4 Sheet, 210mm x 300mm x 3mm` or smaller
* [Hand Cutter Knife](https://www.ebay.com/itm/234282639532?siteid=0) for Acrylic Perspex Plastic sheets mentioned above
* [Handsaw], **must use** [`24 TPI`]() blades, ie. [1](https://olx.ba/artikal/40428806/neo-rucna-pila-za-metal-300-mm-24-tpi-3d-43-300/), [2](https://olx.ba/artikal/59033723), [3](https://adrian5.olx.ba/artikal/69095229)
* A wet dry `wood/metal` Sandpaper's, spec. grits - start with *120-220*, go towards finer grits of *400-600*, and go higher if required
* Use [automotive compund](https://www.ebay.com/itm/112705295295?siteid=0) to bring the edge to shine, and refine transparency details
* [4x Grinding Set with Metal Handles](https://www.ebay.com/itm/322111862108?siteid=0&customid=&toolid=20012)

### Cutting Process

* Youtube: [Tutorial Here](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aLohcEFa850)

In short the steps are as following:

* Do not remove markings or stickers from the original acrylic sheet/cutout
* Cut perspex plastic using handsaw and/or hand cutter knife for detials
* Use [grit](https://www.ebay.com/itm/322111862108?siteid=0)/grinding [1]](https://www.ebay.com/itm/322111862108?siteid=0) set and start grinding the plastic cutout by the edges
* Do a sandpaper gritting begininng with the `120 - 400`, and complete with max: `220-600`) for best result
* Put automotive shing compund on a towler and start using it against the sandpaper side
* Keep using it on sides you want to make more shiny and detailed
* Check the above video for results and instructions
